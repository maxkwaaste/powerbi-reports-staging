6.0 Frequently Asked Questions
What specific data dimensions does a PSA expose that QuickBooks does not?

A PSA like Autotask tracks revenue at nine different billing-item levels (recurring service, subscription, time entry, product, charge, milestone, and adjustments), ties each to a specific contract and client, and records both revenue and internal cost per item. QuickBooks tracks revenue as journal entries with optional categories, but lacks native concepts for contracts, service units, or per-item cost allocation.

How does this report calculate margin per billing category?

Margin is calculated as (Revenue minus Cost) divided by Revenue for each billing item sub_type. Revenue comes from the total_amount field and cost from the our_cost field in the Autotask billing items table. Note that cost here reflects the internal cost recorded in the PSA, not necessarily the full loaded cost including overhead.

Why do time entries show negative margin?

Time entries (sub_type 1) in this dataset generated €274K in billable revenue but carried €1.21M in internal cost. This happens when time is logged against contracts that do not bill hourly (like recurring service or block hours contracts), so the revenue appears under the contract billing type while the cost stays with the time entry.
